From Karur to Dubai

The story nobody tells about moving from a small town to a big stage — raw, honest, and real.

Read Full Post on Blogger ↗ ← All Posts

Karur is a town in Tamil Nadu where everyone knows everyone. Where the air smells like cotton and loom oil, where ambition is considered a strange thing to have in excess.

Dubai is a city that was built to make you feel small — and somehow, also to make you believe anything is possible.

I have stood in both places. And the distance between them is not measured in kilometres.

What Nobody Tells You About Leaving

They show you the destination photos. The skylines. The certificates. The LinkedIn updates that say "Excited to announce..."

Nobody shows you the 2am moments when you wonder if you made the right call. The loneliness of being in a room full of people who do not know your story. The quiet weight of being the first in your circle to do something that has no roadmap.

"The most courageous act is still to think for yourself. Aloud." — Coco Chanel

But here is what is also true: that loneliness? It is a teacher. Those 2am doubts? They forge clarity you cannot get any other way.

The Real Gap

The gap between Karur and Dubai — or anywhere you are trying to go — is not skill. It is not even opportunity, though that matters.

The real gap is the willingness to be uncomfortable long enough for transformation to happen. To hold your identity loosely enough that you can grow into someone new without losing who you actually are.

What I Carried

I carried Karur with me. The work ethic. The directness. The groundedness that comes from knowing where you are from. Those are not disadvantages in a global space — they are anchors.

The full story — including the specific moments that changed everything — is on Blogger. It is the kind of post I wrote for the version of me that needed to read it three years ago.
