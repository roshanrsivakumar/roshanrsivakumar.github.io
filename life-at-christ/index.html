Life at Christ University

Three years of BBA at one of India's premier universities — analytics competitions, flagship events, unforgettable classmates, and the foundation of everything that followed.

The Beginning

From Karur to Bengaluru

Stepping onto the Christ University campus for the first time was unlike anything I had experienced. The sheer scale of the institution, the diversity of students from across India, and the culture of academic excellence — it was all immediately apparent that this was a place that would demand the best from me.

I enrolled in the Bachelor of Business Administration programme, quickly gravitating toward Operations Management and the newly emerging world of Business Analytics. The curriculum was rigorous, the faculty demanding, and the peer group outstanding.

"Christ University didn't just give me a degree — it gave me a way of thinking."

Competition

Chrizellenz 2024 — 3rd Prize

Chrizellenz is Christ University's flagship Business Analytics Competition — a high-stakes event where students tackle real-world datasets and present data-driven strategic recommendations to a panel of industry judges.

In 2024, I competed as part of a team from the TVW cohort. We applied exploratory data analysis, built visualisations in Excel and Python, and constructed a strategy deck that earned us 3rd place.

This competition confirmed that the real power of analytics lies not in the numbers themselves, but in the decisions they unlock.

Events & Leadership

Ushus, Pioneer Club & Beyond

Being part of the organising team at Ushus — Christ University's national-level cultural and management festival — was one of the most intense and rewarding experiences of my undergraduate years.

I was also an active member of the Pioneer Club, involved in event setup, logistics, and student community initiatives. From back-to-school practice sessions with TVW classmates to theatre workshops in late June 2024 — every event added a new dimension to my leadership toolkit.

The Vara Foundation visits and community engagement work gave me a perspective that went far beyond the classroom.

Community

Classmates, Lean Ops & TVW

The TVW cohort was where I found my tribe. A diverse, ambitious group of students who balanced academics with real-world curiosity — our shared experiences stretched from OBT 2024 trips to late-night study sessions before analytics presentations.

My L3 classmates in the Lean Operations and Systems Department were an exceptional group who pushed me to think systems-first. The seeds of my current work at RR Hevirex Coach Builders were planted here.

"The classroom is where you learn the theory. The cohort is where you learn everything else."
