Life at Christ University

MBA in Lean Operations at one of India's premier universities. Analytics competitions, Pioneer Club, Ushus, OBT treks, and friendships that will last a lifetime.

The Beginning

From Karur to Bengaluru

Stepping onto the Christ University campus for the first time was unlike anything I had experienced. The sheer scale of the institution, the diversity of students from across India, and the culture of academic excellence made it immediately clear that this was a place that would demand the best from me.

I enrolled in the MBA in Lean Operations programme, quickly gravitating toward Operations Management and the world of Business Analytics. The curriculum was rigorous, the faculty demanding, and the peer group outstanding.

"Christ University didn't just give me a degree — it gave me a way of thinking."

Competition

Chrizellenz 2024 — 3rd Prize

Chrizellenz is Christ University's flagship Business Analytics Competition, where students tackle real-world datasets and present data-driven strategic recommendations to a panel of industry judges.

In 2024, I competed as part of a team from the TVW cohort. We applied exploratory data analysis, built visualisations, and constructed a strategy deck that earned us 3rd place.

This competition confirmed that the real power of analytics lies not in the numbers themselves, but in the decisions they unlock.

Events & Leadership

Ushus, Pioneer Club & Beyond

Being part of the Ushus organising team — Christ University's national-level cultural and management festival — was one of the most intense and rewarding experiences of my time there. I was present for both days of Ushus 2024.

I was also an active member of the Pioneer Club, involved from the very first event (Curtain Raiser) through event setup, logistics, and community initiatives across the year.

The Vara Foundation volunteering visits gave me a perspective that went far beyond the classroom.

Community

TVW, OBT & the LOS-3 Family

The TVW cohort was where I found my tribe. A diverse, ambitious group whose shared experiences stretched from OBT 2024 treks in Ramanagar to late-night study sessions before analytics presentations.

My LOS-3 classmates in the Lean Operations and Systems Department pushed me to think systems-first. The seeds of my current work at RR Hevirex Coach Builders were planted in those classrooms.

"The classroom is where you learn the theory. The cohort is where you learn everything else."
