Life at Christ University

MBA in Lean Operations, 2024–26. The two years that turned an introvert from Karur into someone who could walk into any room, start any conversation, and build something real.

MBA Lean Operations Pioneer Club Ushus 2024 Organiser Chrizellenz 3rd Prize Basha Utsav OBT Ramanagar

The Transformation

From Introvert to Leader

When I arrived at Christ University from Karur, I was quiet. I kept to myself. I would observe before I spoke, and most of the time, I didn't speak at all. That was who I was — an introvert who had mastered the art of staying invisible.

Christ changed that. Not overnight, but steadily. The MBA in Lean Operations pushed me into group projects, presentations, event organisation, and rooms full of people from every corner of India. I had no choice but to come out of my shell — and once I did, I never went back.

I started talking to people I would never have approached. I built connections I still rely on today. I discovered that the difference between an introvert and a leader is usually just one decision — the decision to show up.

"Christ University didn't just teach me Lean Operations — it taught me how to be in a room with people and make something happen."
First day at Christ University | Bengaluru, 2024

The Curriculum

Lean Operations, Marketing & Finance

The MBA programme gave me three things I use every single day. Lean Operations — the discipline of eliminating waste, designing efficient systems, and measuring what matters. This directly shapes how I run production at RR Hevirex Coach Builders.

Marketing — I understood for the first time how to build a brand, communicate value, and think from a customer's perspective. That thinking feeds directly into myThrivehood and every piece of content I create.

Financial costing — enough to read, understand, and manage the accounting documentation of a manufacturing business. I came in not knowing a balance sheet. I left knowing how to use one to make decisions.

MBA coursework | Christ University, Bengaluru

Outbound Training

OBT Ramanagar — Reading People

The Outbound Training at Ramanagar was my first experience spending real time with my classmates outside the classroom. For a former introvert, it was everything.

I used it as an exercise in reading people. Away from formal settings, everyone's personality comes out — who leads, who follows, who thrives under pressure, who shuts down. I observed all of it deliberately, because I knew these were the people I would be building group projects with for the next two years.

That trip gave me something no classroom could: a map of my cohort. I knew who to pair with for what, how to navigate conflict before it started, and how to get the best out of the people around me.

Basha Utsav — Ethnic Day

Mini India in One Campus

Christ University is not just a university — it is a small India. Students arrive from every state, every language, every tradition. Basha Utsav, the annual ethnic cultural day, is where that diversity becomes visible all at once.

I represented South India — in traditional attire, with our food, our music, our language. But more than that, I spent the day learning from people representing cultures I had never encountered. I sat with students from the Northeast, from Bengal, from Rajasthan, and understood for the first time how differently people think, celebrate, and build community.

That single day widened my world more than I expected. The ability to connect across culture, to find common ground with someone completely different from you — that is a leadership skill. Christ gave it to me for free.

Basha Utsav | Representing South India | Christ University, Bengaluru

Pioneer Club

Where I Found Event Management

Pioneer Club was where theory met reality. I joined from the very first event — Curtain Raiser — and stayed involved through stage setup, logistics, promotions, and execution. Every event was a live case study in operations management.

Working alongside a diverse team taught me something the MBA curriculum doesn't cover: how to get things done with people who have different working styles, different priorities, and different definitions of "done." That skill is irreplaceable.

Pioneer Club also sparked my interest in media marketing and digital marketing. Directing the BTS promo for a flagship event, thinking about how to capture and communicate what was happening — I realised I genuinely enjoy that side of things. It feeds directly into everything I do with myThrivehood today.

Chrizellenz → Ushus

Participant. Then Organiser.

Chrizellenz 2024 is Christ University's flagship Business Analytics Competition. I entered as a participant with my team — the Dawgs — and we earned 3rd place with our data-driven strategy presentation. That performance got noticed.

The result of winning at Chrizellenz was an invitation to join the Ushus 2024 organising team. Ushus is Christ University's national-level cultural and management festival — one of the biggest student-run events in South India.

Going from competitor to organiser in the same year taught me what progression looks like when you put in the work. You don't stay in the audience. You earn your way to the other side of the table.
