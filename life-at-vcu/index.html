Life at VCU

Ten months, 7,000 miles from Karur earning an MBA in Decision Analytics | Virginia Commonwealth University, finding a second family, and proving that a little discomfort makes all the difference.

Leaving Bangalore for Richmond

When I boarded that flight from Bangalore to Richmond, Virginia, I carried more than luggage I carried a decade of ambition. I had already completed a degree in Mechanical Engineering in India, but something in me needed to be tested in a completely new environment, 7,000 miles from everyone I knew.

The MBA in Decision Analytics at Virginia Commonwealth University was exactly that test. Ten months of grinding sleepless nights, deadlines that felt impossible, and missing family every single day. But no regrets. Not one.

American Marketing Association Best Spokesperson

One of my proudest moments at VCU was joining the American Marketing Association chapter and diving in headfirst. I didn't just attend meetings I competed, presented, and pushed myself into uncomfortable rooms on purpose. As one of just three graduate students in a chapter dominated by undergraduates, I had to earn my place.

At the Fall Induction Ceremony, I was awarded Best Spokesperson recognition that meant more to me because I had earned it in a country and culture that wasn't my own.

The AMA taught me that marketing isn't a function it's a mindset. That lesson has stayed with me every day since.

Best Spokesperson AMA Fall Induction

The Professor Who Changed How I Think

Every student has one professor who doesn't just teach a subject they teach a way of thinking. For me, that was Professor James Eggleston at VCU.

His approach to Six Sigma and operations management wasn't textbook recitation. He challenged you to find the inefficiency, diagnose the root cause, and fix it with data. His classes were relentless and precise the kind that make you uncomfortable in the best possible way.

Professor Eggleston shaped how I approach operational problems today as COO at RR Hevirex Coach Builders. The discipline he instilled in me measure twice, cut once, then measure again shows up in every process I design.

With Professor James Eggleston | VCU

Desk Assistant: Work, Responsibility & Growth

I didn't come to VCU just to study. I worked as a Desk Assistant in student residential housing managing check-ins, supporting residents, and keeping things running smoothly, often late into the night after full days of class.

It was humbling and energising at the same time. The job gave me financial independence, deepened my understanding of campus operations, and introduced me to hundreds of students from every corner of the world.

Coming from a management background in India, the experience of doing the work yourself not just delegating it permanently expanded how I think about leadership.

Graduation Day

Standing in that auditorium in Richmond, in my graduation gown, I thought about the night I landed in the US not knowing a single person within a 7,000-mile radius. I thought about the nights I cooked at 1am after a shift, and the mornings I showed up to 8am lectures running on three hours of sleep.

Every sacrifice the distance from family, the financial pressure, the emotional weight of being alone made the moment of crossing that stage infinitely sweeter. The MSc from VCU isn't just a qualification. It's evidence of what happens when you refuse to quit.
